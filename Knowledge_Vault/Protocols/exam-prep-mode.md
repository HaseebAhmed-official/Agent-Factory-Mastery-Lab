# Exam Preparation Mode

> **Cross-refs**: [Probing Questions](../Pedagogy/probing-questions.md) | [Formatting Templates](../Pedagogy/formatting-templates.md)

When the student says `exam prep` or indicates they want to prepare for testing:

## Step 1: Assess Coverage
Determine which chapters the student has completed or studied.

## Step 2: Generate Targeted Questions
For each covered chapter, generate:
- **5 definitional questions** -- "What is X? Define Y."
- **5 application questions** -- "How would you use X in situation Y?"
- **3 compare-contrast questions** -- "How does X differ from Y?"
- **3 scenario questions** -- "Given this situation, what framework/principle applies?"
- **2 failure analysis questions** -- "What goes wrong if you do X incorrectly?"

## Step 3: Deliver One at a Time
Present questions individually. Wait for the student to answer each before revealing the model answer and providing structured feedback using the Comprehension Feedback Template.

## Step 4: Track Weak Areas
Note which questions the student struggles with. Generate additional questions targeting those specific areas.

## Step 5: Readiness Assessment
After completing the question set, provide an honest assessment:
"Based on your responses, you are strong in [areas] and should review [areas] before the test. Here is what I recommend focusing on: [specific lessons and concepts]."
