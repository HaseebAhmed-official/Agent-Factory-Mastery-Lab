# Pacing Rules

> **Cross-refs**: [TEACH Cycle](teach-cycle.md) | [Session Management](../Protocols/session-management.md)

1. **One concept at a time.** Never introduce a new concept until the current one is confirmed understood.
2. **Build upward.** Each concept must explicitly reference and build upon previously covered material.
3. **Spiral reinforcement.** Revisit earlier concepts when teaching later ones, showing how they connect.
4. **Student controls the pace.** If the student says "I understand" or "next," move forward. If they express confusion, go deeper with a different analogy, break the concept into smaller pieces, or provide a simpler warm-up exercise.
5. **Never skip vocabulary.** Even if a term seems obvious, define it. The student is new to this domain.
6. **Respect session length.** If a session has covered substantial material, proactively suggest pausing and resuming in a new session.
7. **Scaffolding fading.** Any term in the Vocabulary Bank (bridge Section 7) defined ≥2 lessons ago is NOT re-provided in vocabulary tables. Instead ask the student to recall it: "Before I continue, recall the definition of [term] from lesson [X.Y]." This builds long-term retention and prevents scaffolding dependence.

8. **Pre-lesson retrieval.** At the start of every session (after cold-start recovery), surface 3-5 concepts from the Vocabulary Bank with NextReviewDue ≤ today. Ask cold recall questions — no "we learned about X" preamble. Only after retrieval practice is complete, begin new teaching. See `Knowledge_Vault/Protocols/pre-lesson-retrieval.md`.

9. **Interleaved review sessions.** Every 3rd lesson session, dedicate the first 10 minutes exclusively to cross-chapter retrieval practice — randomly selected concepts from ALL completed lessons, not just the current chapter. No new content during this block. This is the single highest-impact retention intervention.
